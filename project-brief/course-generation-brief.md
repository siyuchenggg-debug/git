# Course Generation Brief

## Master Source

The master course design is stored in:

`source/中信设计中亚商务洽谈英语课程设计.xlsx`

This Excel file is the primary source for generating all course materials.

When generating lessons, handouts, slides, listening scripts or role-play tasks, follow the structure and logic in the Excel file.

## Course Positioning

This is a one-on-one executive Business English course for a senior business development / management professional from CITIC Design’s Seventh Design Institute.

The course focuses on Central Asian urban design consulting and business negotiation.

The main case is the Almaty Sairan CBD Manhattan urban design project.

The course should also help the learner transfer the communication framework to other Central Asian projects, including AI Park, EXPO, industrial parks, public buildings, urban renewal projects and Shymkent-related opportunities.

## Course Logic

The course is not a generic Business English course.

It should follow this logic:

typical case input → language framework → controlled practice → transfer practice → realistic simulation → personal phrase bank.

Each lesson should combine listening and speaking.

## Six-Lesson Structure

Lesson 01:
Executive introduction, company profile and Central Asian project roadmap.

Lesson 02:
Understanding the client brief and project requirements.

Lesson 03:
Explaining the integrated strategy-and-planning approach.

Lesson 04:
Designer-to-designer communication on the concept scheme.

Lesson 05:
Technical clarification and design refinement.

Lesson 06:
Full project meeting simulation and personal phrase bank.

## Listening-Speaking Loop

Each lesson should follow this training loop:

1. Warm-up / review
2. Listening input
3. Listening check
4. Language focus
5. Controlled speaking
6. Role-play / simulation
7. Feedback and improved output
8. Homework

The listening materials should be realistic project conversations, not generic textbook dialogues.

## Textbook Integration

Use the two textbooks in this proportion:

1. English for Planning and Building Professionals: about 60%
   - main course logic
   - project process
   - briefing
   - feasibility study
   - concept design
   - planning and building professional communication

2. Cambridge English for Engineering: about 25%
   - technical requirements
   - drawings
   - dimensions
   - design phases
   - feasibility
   - redesigns
   - technical clarification

3. CITIC Design and Central Asian project materials: about 15%
   - real project context
   - Almaty Sairan CBD
   - AI Park
   - EXPO
   - Shymkent
   - business expansion scenarios

## Required Output for Each Lesson

For each lesson, generate:

1. detailed lesson plan
2. teacher’s notes
3. learner handout
4. listening script
5. listening task sheet
6. speaking drills
7. role-play cards
8. slide outline
9. homework
10. personal phrase bank items

## Style Requirements

The course should be:

- executive-level
- business-task-oriented
- suitable for one-on-one teaching
- closely related to urban planning, architecture, design consulting and Central Asian market development
- focused on practical listening and speaking
- professional but not overly academic

## Avoid

Do not generate generic Business English content.

Do not limit the course to the Sairan CBD project only.

Do not turn the course into vocabulary memorization.

Do not use childish classroom activities.

Do not fabricate legal, tax or regulatory conclusions.

When mentioning contracts, tax, local regulations or statutory procedures, state that the material is for communication training only and should be reviewed by legal, tax or locally registered professionals before actual use.
